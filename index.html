📌 Key concepts

Cognitive load theory (Sweller, 1988) describes the limited capacity of working memory when learning new information.

Three types: intrinsic, extraneous and germane cognitive load — each with different implications for learning effectiveness.

Automatization reduces cognitive load by building knowledge schemas in long-term memory.

📖 Glossary

Cognitive load — The mental effort being used in working memory at any given time.

Schema — A cognitive framework that helps organize and interpret information.

Automatization — A process by which a behavior becomes automatic through practice, freeing up cognitive resources.

FAQ

Common questions.

What file types does StudySnap support? +

StudySnap currently supports PDF, Word (.docx), and plain text paste. Support for PowerPoint slides and scanned documents (via OCR) is planned for Q3 2025.

Is my data safe and private? +

Yes. Your documents are processed securely and never used to train AI models. We comply with GDPR. Documents are deleted from our servers after processing unless you choose to save them.

How accurate are the summaries? +

StudySnap uses state-of-the-art language models and achieves very high accuracy on academic texts. We always recommend reviewing the summary alongside your original material for critical exams.

Does it work for all subjects? +

Yes — StudySnap works well for text-heavy subjects like Law, Psychology, Economics, History, Medicine and more. It works less well for highly mathematical content (like pure math or physics equations), though support for that is in development.

Can I share summaries with my study group? +

Yes. Pro users can share a read-only link to any summary — no account needed to view it. Study Group plan users can also work on shared documents together in real time.

What languages does it support? +

StudySnap works with documents in English, Dutch, German, French and Spanish. You can also set your preferred output language independently of the input language.
